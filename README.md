# MDrive — UMich Student Carpooling Platform

**Type:** Team Project

## Overview
Led customer discovery and two live market experiments to validate demand for a peer-to-peer carpooling platform connecting University of Michigan students without cars to student drivers running errands. Validated the core value proposition with strong behavioral evidence from 42 on-campus interviews.

## Problem
Undergraduate students without cars struggled to get off campus affordably to run errands — groceries, furniture, career fair prep. Existing options (Uber, campus bus) were too expensive or inconvenient. Students with cars had no easy way to offset costs while running their own errands.

## Solution
**MDrive** — a software platform driven by students, for students. Students without cars hitch a ride with fellow UMich students running errands. Students with cars earn money doing what they were already doing.

## Experiment 1 — Concierge Test
- Built two Google Forms (rider + driver) and distributed QR code flyers across campus
- **12 rider responses** — willingness to pay $5-$50
- **4 driver responses** — minimum acceptable price $10-$15
- Ran a live grocery run with 3 undergrads, generating **$15 real revenue** at $5/person

## Experiment 2 — Chai Chats
- On-campus customer discovery event offering free chai to engage students
- Interviewed **42 undergraduate students**
- **92%** of students with cars expressed willingness to drive peers for compensation
- **76%** of students without cars reported a regular need to leave campus (93% including "sometimes")
- Key friction points identified: safety concerns, scheduling, awkwardness with strangers

## Validated Metrics
| Metric | Result |
|---|---|
| Driver willingness rate | 92% |
